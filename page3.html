Birth Month Flowers Part 1

When it comes to birth month flowers most months will have a primary and secondary flower due to different cultures or availability. Birth flowers are silimar to Birthstones in the sense that they are designated to a certain month, typically based on the season they are in bloom.

January

The Primary flower for the month of January is a Carnation, with the secondary flower being a Snowdrop. Carnations are known for their popular use During Mother's day and Valentine's Day due to their meaning relating to love and rememberance. On the other hand Snowdrops symbolize sympathy or are used for celebration used to bring hope and optimism.

February

rThe Primary flower for the month of February is a Violet, with the seconday flower being a Primrose. Violets are symbolic of faithfulness, everlasting love and loyalty. Violets are even used as a symbolic symbol in some religions. The Primrose was a symbol for young love or "I can't live without you". They were said to provide protection and safety.

March

TThe Primary flower for the month of March is a Daffodil, with the secondary flower being a Jonquil. With March comes the beginning of spring, meaning Daffodil's represent rebirth or new beginnings. Another meaning Daffodil's have is forgivness or creativity.
